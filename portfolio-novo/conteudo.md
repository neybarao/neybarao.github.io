## Projeto: AL5 Bank

- **Ano:** 2018 · **Cliente:** AL5 Bank · **Serviços:** UI Design, UX Design
- **Título:** Reducing Barriers to Account Creation

### Project Overview
**Goal** — The AL5 Bank Mobile App Redesign was all about making the app work better for its main users—farmers and agribusiness owners. The goal was to give the app a complete refresh, making it easier to use, more intuitive, and better suited to their specific financial needs.

**Problem** — The original AL5 Bank app didn't quite hit the mark for its users, especially when it came to handling financial transactions and tracking information specific to the agro-industry. Because of this, a full redesign was needed to better meet their needs.

### Research and Analysis
- **Desk Research** — Using Firebase Analytics, reviews, and support data, we identified user behavior, key issues, and areas for improvement.
- **Usability Analysis** — The analysis showed key issues in the user journey, like tricky navigation and a cluttered interface that made things harder than they should be.
- **Competitor Analysis** — Looking at competitor apps showed us what works well in the financial sector, helping us set design benchmarks for the redesign.

### Key Findings
1. **Onboarding Process Flaws** — A significant glitch in the onboarding process was preventing user sign-up completion, affecting user acquisition.
2. **Form Field Confusion** — User feedback revealed confusion and redundancy in certain sign-up form fields.
3. **Technical Issues and Missing Information** — Users reported frequent technical glitches and a lack of essential transaction details.

### Design Process
1. **Ideation** — The project's direction was informed by heuristic adjustments rather than broad ideation, focusing on resolving the identified issues.
2. **Prototyping** — We used Figma to build high-fidelity prototypes, leveraging an existing design library to speed up the process and focus on solutions.
3. **User Testing** — Moderated testing with 10 participants revealed misunderstandings caused by financial jargon and complex terms, impacting user comprehension during the sign-up process.
4. **Iteration** — Significant changes were implemented, including simplifying the app's copy to remove jargon, eliminating unnecessary fields, and introducing explanation screens for complex actions.

### Outcome and Impact
- **Client Reception** — Initially skeptical about the necessity of a UX designer, AL5 Bank was convinced of the value brought by a user-centered design approach after seeing the redesign's results, leading to the permanent inclusion of a UX designer in the development team.
- **User Experience Improvements** — Post-launch, there was a dramatic decrease in Customer Support tickets related to the onboarding process, indicating a significant enhancement in the user experience and operational efficiency for AL5 Bank.

### Key Metrics
- **48%** — Reduction in customer support calls
- **49%** — Success rate increase on sign up
- **0** — Days to open a bank account

## Projeto: Cook Mate

- **Ano:** 2013 · **Cliente:** Cook Mate · **Serviços:** UI Design, UX Design
- **Título:** Talk & Cook: Designing a Hands-Free Start for Newbie Chefs

### Project Overview
**Goal** — Cook Mate aims to empower rookie cooks by providing an easy-to-use app that leverages modern technology to simplify the cooking process.

**Problem** — Users frequently face challenges in finding recipes that align with their skill level, dietary preferences, and the ingredients they have on hand.

### Research and Analysis
- **Desk Research** — Conducted a thorough analysis of top cooking apps like Yummly, Tasty, Kitchen Stories, and SuperCook. This comparative study highlighted their strengths and weaknesses, informing the design of Cook Mate to better address user needs.
- **Demographics & Preferences** — Through research, I identified the pain points and motivations of new cooks, pinpointing the target audience for the app. This insight will guide features that resonate with users.
- **Personas** — From the target audience analysis, I developed two proto personas that encapsulate the key characteristics and behaviors of typical users, providing a clearer direction for the app's design.

### Key Findings
1. **Competitor Insights** — Existing recipe apps lack non-vegetarian variety, have poor filtering, and fail to recommend recipes that match available ingredients. Many also lack ingredient substitution features, making it hard for users to adapt recipes. Cook Mate aims to fill these gaps with a more intuitive and flexible experience for beginners.
2. **User Insights** — Most users are women (80%), prioritize healthy eating (56%), and seek budget-friendly meals (67%). With 60% cooking at home but preferring quick recipes, Cook Mate needs to offer simple, cost-effective, and time-efficient cooking guidance.

### Design Process
**01 Ideation**

_Finding the Right Recipes_
- **Ingredient-Based Search** — Users input ingredients they have, and the app suggests recipes that match.
- **Skill Level Filtering** — Recipes are categorized as beginner, intermediate, or advanced, helping users find recipes that match their experience.
- **Dietary & Allergy Filters** — Users can quickly filter recipes by dietary restrictions (vegan, gluten-free, nut-free, etc.).

_Guided Cooking Experience_
- **Step-by-Step Interactive Guides** — Recipes include photos, videos, and clear instructions, making it easier for beginners to follow.
- **Voice-Guided Cooking** — A hands-free assistant reads instructions and sets timers, allowing users to focus on cooking.
- **Progress Tracker** — Users can mark steps as completed, keeping them organized and reducing mistakes.

**02 Prototyping** — With no existing design library, I started with hand-drawn wireframes to quickly validate ideas. This allowed for rapid iteration before committing to digital designs. Next, I researched visual styles inspired by food-related designs and recipe books to define the app's look and feel. Once the style direction was clear, I moved to Figma to create a component library for consistency across the UI. Finally, I developed high-fidelity prototypes in Figma, ensuring the design was polished and ready for testing.

### Project Outcomes & Conclusion
Despite thorough research, ideation, and high-fidelity prototyping, Cook Mate's development was paused due to project limitations, and the product was never launched.

However, the process provided valuable insights into beginner cooks' needs, highlighting the importance of personalized recipe recommendations, guided cooking experiences, and accessible filtering options. The research and design work conducted serve as a strong foundation for potential future development or application in similar projects.

This case study reinforced the importance of validating user needs early, adapting to constraints, and building scalable design systems that can be leveraged even if a project doesn't move forward. Every design challenge contributes to better problem-solving skills and deeper user understanding, making it a valuable experience for future UX work.

## Projeto: Senac

- **Ano:** 2018 · **Cliente:** SENAC · **Serviços:** UI Design, UX Design
- **Título:** Digital Transformation of SENAC's Enrollment Process

### Project Overview
**Goal** — The goal was to make it easier for students to find and enroll in courses online, streamlining the process and improving access to education.

**Problem** — Senac faced overcrowding and inconsistent enrollment nationwide. The project created an online platform to streamline the process.

### Research and Analysis
- **Shadowing** — Exploratory research involved observing the enrollment process and identifying pain points of Senac's secretaries.
- **User Interviews** — Interviews with students were conducted to understand their experiences with the enrollment process.

### Key Findings
1. **Time constraint** — Senac's office hours clashed with students' work schedules, making enrollment difficult.
2. **Technology limitation** — Some students didn't have a computer at home, so they had to go to Senac's offices to enroll.
3. **Lack of process consistency** — A big challenge was standardizing registration across Brazil's 28 states, each with different requirements.

### Design Process
- The redesign focused on addressing the identified technical and usability issues through targeted design and content strategy adjustments.
1. **Ideation** — The project's direction was shaped by extensive ideation, focusing on delivering smart solutions and leveraging Senac's existing intelligence technologies.
2. **Prototyping** — We used Figma to build high-fidelity prototypes, creating a design library based on Senac's brand guidelines.
3. **User Testing** — Moderated testing with 10 students, split into two rounds, highlighted the need for usability improvements in the tested prototype.
4. **Iteration** — Significant changes were made, including simplifying the web app's copy to eliminate jargon and addressing minor usability issues.

### Project Outcome
1. **Implementation** — Senac's team launched a marketing and communication campaign to introduce students and candidates to the new online enrollment option.
2. **Impact** — Since there was no prior platform, we lacked comparative data, but we noticed notably fewer crowds at the in-person enrollment office.
3. **Operational Impact** — The online platform launched, instantly easing admin work. Students adopted it fast, proving it simplifies enrollment and boosts access for Brazil's adult learners.
