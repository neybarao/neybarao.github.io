# AL5 Bank, Reducing barriers to account creation

> **A ground-up redesign that cut onboarding-related support tickets by 48% and made opening an account effortless for farmers and agribusiness owners.**

## Overview

AL5 Bank serves a specialized audience: farmers and agribusiness owners with financial needs that generic banking apps don't address. The original app fell short exactly where it mattered most, in handling transactions and tracking information specific to the agro-industry.

**The challenge.** The app wasn't meeting its users, and a glitch-ridden onboarding flow was actively losing sign-ups. AL5 Bank needed a full redesign: easier to use, more intuitive, and built around how its users actually work.

## My role

As **Lead Designer**, I owned research and design direction end to end, from desk research and usability analysis through high-fidelity prototyping, moderated testing, and iteration. I also made the internal case for user-centered design to a skeptical client.

## Process

### Research
I grounded the redesign in evidence before touching the interface:
- **Desk research** using Firebase Analytics, app-store reviews, and support data to map real user behavior and recurring issues.
- **Usability analysis** of the existing app to locate friction in the journey.
- **Competitor analysis** of financial apps to set clear design benchmarks.

### Key findings
1. **Broken onboarding.** A glitch in the sign-up flow prevented users from completing registration, directly hurting acquisition.
2. **Confusing forms.** Users found certain sign-up fields redundant and unclear.
3. **Missing information.** Frequent technical glitches and a lack of essential transaction details eroded trust.

### Design decisions
Because the problems were well-defined, I led with heuristic, targeted fixes rather than broad ideation, solving known issues instead of redesigning for novelty. The key moves:
- **Cut the jargon.** Financial terms were confusing users mid-sign-up, so I simplified the copy to plain language.
- **Removed unnecessary fields** to shorten the path to an open account.
- **Added explanation screens** for complex actions, so users always understood what they were doing and why.

I prototyped in Figma, leveraging an existing design library to move fast and keep focus on the solutions rather than reinventing components.

### Testing & iteration
Moderated testing with 10 participants confirmed the core problem: financial jargon and complex terms were breaking comprehension during sign-up. Those insights drove the iteration above, simpler copy, fewer fields, clearer guidance.

## Outcome & impact

Initially skeptical that a UX designer was even necessary, AL5 Bank changed its mind after seeing the results, and made a UX designer a permanent part of the development team. Post-launch, support tickets tied to onboarding dropped sharply, signaling a real gain in both user experience and operational efficiency.

- **48%:** reduction in customer-support calls
- **49%:** increase in sign-up success rate
- **0 days:** to open a bank account
