# Cook Mate, A hands-free start for newbie chefs

> **A concept app that helps rookie cooks find recipes from what's already in the kitchen, and follow them hands-free, step by step.**

_Original title: "Talk & Cook: Designing a Hands-Free Start for Newbie Chefs"._

## Overview

Cook Mate set out to empower rookie cooks with an app that uses modern technology to make cooking simpler. New cooks consistently struggle to find recipes that match their skill level, dietary preferences, and, crucially, the ingredients they already have on hand.

**The challenge.** Existing recipe apps assume you know what to make and have everything you need. Beginners don't. Cook Mate had to meet people where they actually are: limited skills, limited time, and whatever's in the fridge.

## My role

This was a solo, self-directed project. I owned the full process: competitive research, audience definition, personas, ideation, visual direction, a component library, and high-fidelity prototypes.

## Process

### Research
- **Desk research.** A thorough analysis of leading cooking apps (Yummly, Tasty, Kitchen Stories, SuperCook), comparing strengths and weaknesses to inform Cook Mate's direction.
- **Demographics & preferences.** Identified the pain points and motivations of new cooks to pinpoint the target audience.
- **Personas.** Developed two proto-personas capturing the key behaviors of typical users, giving the design a clear direction.

### Key findings
1. **Competitor gaps.** Existing apps lacked non-vegetarian variety, had poor filtering, and failed to recommend recipes based on available ingredients or offer substitutions. A clear opening for a more flexible, beginner-friendly experience.
2. **User insights.** Most users were women (80%), prioritized healthy eating (56%), and sought budget-friendly meals (67%). With 60% cooking at home but preferring quick recipes, the app had to be simple, cost-effective, and time-efficient.

### Design decisions
I shaped the feature set around two jobs beginners actually need help with.

**Finding the right recipe:**
- **Ingredient-based search.** Users enter what they have; the app suggests matching recipes.
- **Skill-level filtering.** Recipes tagged beginner, intermediate, or advanced.
- **Dietary & allergy filters.** Quick filtering for vegan, gluten-free, nut-free, and more.

**Following it confidently:**
- **Step-by-step interactive guides.** Photos, video, and clear instructions.
- **Voice-guided cooking.** A hands-free assistant reads steps and sets timers, so users keep their hands on the food.
- **Progress tracker.** Mark steps complete to stay organized and avoid mistakes.

### Prototyping
With no existing design library, I started with hand-drawn wireframes to validate ideas fast before committing to digital. I then researched visual styles from food design and recipe books to set the look and feel, built a component library in Figma for consistency, and produced polished high-fidelity prototypes ready for testing.

## Outcome & impact

Despite thorough research, ideation, and high-fidelity prototyping, Cook Mate was paused due to project limitations and never launched. What it produced was a clear, validated foundation: a strong understanding of beginner cooks' needs and a complete design system that could power future development or similar products.

## Reflection

This project reinforced what carries into every engagement: validate user needs early, adapt to real-world constraints, and build scalable design systems that hold value even when a product doesn't ship. Not every project launches, but every one sharpens the problem-solving.
